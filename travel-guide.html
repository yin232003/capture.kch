Travel Guide

Practical Essentials for Your Kuching Journey

Getting Around

Taxis

RM15–25 within city

Traditional metered taxis available at major locations

Grab

Usually cheaper

Convenient ride-hailing app widely used in Kuching

River Ferry

RM19 to cross

Traditional way to cross Sarawak River

Day Trips

RM20–150

Shared vans to Bako or private car hire

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season

March – October

Best for hiking and wildlife spotting. Lower rainfall means better trail conditions and more active animals.

Recommended for: Outdoor activities, national park visits, beach trips

Wet Season

November – February

Afternoon showers are common but brief. Lush green landscapes and fewer tourists. Indoor attractions remain accessible.

Recommended for: Cultural sites, museums, food tours, budget travelers

Major Festivals

Rainforest World Music Festival

When: July

Three-day celebration of world music at Sarawak Cultural Village. International and indigenous performers.

Sarawak Cultural Festival

When: September

Showcase of indigenous cultures, traditional dances, handicrafts, and local cuisine.

Essential Tips

Currency & Payment

Malaysian Ringgit (RM). ATMs widely available. Many street vendors prefer cash.

Language

Malay is official, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas and hotels.

Climate

Tropical year-round: 24–32°C. Pack light, breathable clothing and rain gear.

Safety

Kuching is very safe. Standard precautions apply. Respect local customs and dress modestly at religious sites.

Connectivity

Free WiFi in most hotels and cafes. SIM cards available at airport (RM30–50).

Healthcare

Good medical facilities. Sarawak General Hospital and private clinics available. Travel insurance recommended.
